01 / 06
Retail Sales Forecasting — Rossmann Stores

End-to-end sales forecasting pipeline on 1M+ rows of German pharmacy chain data. Engineered calendar, lag, and rolling-window features, then compared Linear Regression, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting regressors using time-series cross-validation.

PythonRandom ForestGradient BoostingTime-Series CVFeature EngineeringPlotly
Source Code ↗
Key Result

Gradient Boosting outperformed Linear Regression baseline; log-transformed Sales target improved model stability. Lag features and rolling averages captured weekly seasonality patterns.

Business Insights
  • Promotions drove measurably higher sales — stores running Promo had significantly elevated daily revenue, validating promotional investment.
  • Nearby competitors did not necessarily hurt sales — stores with very close competition sometimes outperformed, suggesting that retail clusters attract more foot traffic overall.
  • Day-of-week patterns were consistent and predictable — Monday sales consistently lagged while mid-week drove the most volume, informing staffing and inventory decisions.
  • Store Type B (larger format) generated the highest average sales, suggesting format expansion could be a high-ROI strategic move.
02 / 06
SMS Spam Detection — NLP Classification

Multi-model spam classifier trained on the UCI SMS Spam Collection. Applied TF-IDF vectorization and benchmarked four algorithms — Logistic Regression, Naïve Bayes, Decision Tree, and KNN — using precision, recall, and F1-score.

NLPTF-IDFLogistic RegressionNaïve BayesKNNDecision Tree
Source Code ↗
Key Result

Comparative analysis across 4 classifiers on 5,574 messages. EDA uncovered clear distinctions: spam messages are longer and lexically distinct from ham (e.g. "free", "win", "claim").

Business Insights
  • Spam messages are significantly longer on average — length alone is a useful low-cost signal that can be used as a fast pre-filter before running expensive models.
  • High-frequency spam words ("free", "win", "cash", "claim") form a small, stable vocabulary — a simple keyword blocklist would catch a large portion of spam at near-zero compute cost.
  • Naïve Bayes delivered competitive accuracy with the fastest training time, making it the practical choice for real-time SMS filtering at scale.
  • False negatives (missed spam) cost users attention; false positives (blocked ham) cost trust — the model can be tuned toward higher recall or precision depending on business priorities.
03 / 06
Building Energy Efficiency — Regression Modeling

Predicted building Heating Load from 8 architectural features (UCI Energy Efficiency dataset). Implemented and systematically compared Linear Regression, Polynomial Regression (degree=2), and Ridge Regression with GridSearchCV hyperparameter tuning.

Linear RegressionPolynomial RegressionRidge RegressionGridSearchCVStandardScalerKFold CV
Source Code ↗
Key Result

Ridge Regression on polynomial features achieved the best test R² and RMSE. Bias-variance trade-off curve showed degree-3+ caused overfitting; optimal alpha found via 5-fold cross-validation.

Business Insights
  • Overall Height and Roof Area were the strongest predictors of Heating Load — architects can prioritize these two design variables to meaningfully reduce a building's energy demand.
  • Glazing Area had a positive relationship with heating load — larger window surfaces increase heat loss, a direct trade-off between natural light and energy efficiency.
  • Relative Compactness was highly inversely correlated with energy use — more compact building shapes retain heat better, a finding that directly informs sustainable architecture guidelines.
  • Orientation showed the weakest impact on heating load, suggesting energy codes could deprioritize orientation requirements in favor of material and structural standards.

06 / 06
House Price Prediction — Regularized Regression

End-to-end house price prediction pipeline on the Ames Housing dataset. Engineered 80+ features including ordinal quality mappings, one-hot encoded neighborhood and style variables, and log-transformed the target to handle price skewness. Compared Lasso and Ridge regression with 5-fold cross-validated alpha tuning.

Lasso / RidgeLassoCV / RidgeCVLog TransformationOne-Hot EncodingFeature ScalingEDA
Source Code ↗
Key Result

Ridge achieved R² of 0.90 on log-transformed sale prices. Log transformation reduced the impact of extreme luxury outliers and improved model stability across both regularization methods.

Business Insights
  • Overall quality rating was the single strongest price driver — a one standard deviation increase in quality score translated to a significant percentage lift in sale price, giving appraisers a quantifiable basis for renovation ROI.
  • Above-ground living area had a near-linear relationship with price, but luxury outliers above 4,000 sq ft distorted predictions — removing them improved accuracy, showing extreme properties need separate valuation models.
  • Neighborhood was a major price determinant even after controlling for size and quality — location effects validate why real estate agents emphasize "location, location, location."
  • Lasso's automatic feature selection zeroed out irrelevant variables — directly valuable for streamlining appraisal checklists and reducing data collection costs.
  • Log-transforming sale price reduced prediction error in dollar terms — a modeling decision with direct financial impact when errors compound across large property portfolios.
